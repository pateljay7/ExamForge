# ExamForge

**Turn any study material into a graded practice exam — in minutes, not hours.**

ExamForge takes the material you're studying (or teaching) — notes, chapters, reference
docs — and uses AI to turn it straight into a multiple-choice exam you can take, get
scored on, and retake to track your progress.

## The problem

Writing good practice questions takes time most learners and educators don't have.
Static question banks go stale, don't match *your* material, and don't adapt to how much
weight each topic actually deserves. ExamForge removes that bottleneck: give it your
content, tell it how the topics should be weighted, and get a fair, well-structured exam
back — ready to take immediately or refine first.

## Who it's for

- **Learners** studying for a certification, class, or interview who want to
  self-test on their own notes instead of generic question banks.
- **Educators and trainers** who need to spin up quizzes from course material and control
  exactly who can create content vs. who can only take it.
- **Teams** running internal knowledge checks — onboarding material, compliance content,
  product training — where different people need different levels of access.

## How it works

1. **Paste your content.** Break it into one or more sections — a chapter, a topic, a
   document — and assign each a weight. A section weighted 70% produces roughly 70% of
   the final question set.
2. **Set the shape of the exam.** Pick difficulty, how many questions you want, whether
   it's timed (a hard countdown that auto-submits, or a simple stopwatch for reference),
   and whether questions/answers should shuffle per attempt to discourage memorization.
3. **Review before you publish.** Every exam starts as a draft. Read through the
   AI-generated questions, hand-edit any of them, or ask the AI to regenerate a single
   question you're not happy with — it's told to avoid repeating what's already there.
   Nothing is available to take until you publish it.
4. **Take it — or share it.** Publish and take the exam yourself, or turn on sharing to
   hand a link to teammates or students so they can take it too.
5. **See exactly where you stand.** Results show your score, time taken, a breakdown by
   content section (so you know *which* topic needs more work, not just your overall
   grade), and every question with the correct answer revealed. Export to CSV or print a
   PDF copy. Retake anytime — every attempt is kept, so your progress is a trend, not a
   single number.

## What makes it flexible

**Choose your AI.** ExamForge isn't locked to one model provider — generate questions
with Claude or Gemini, and switch between them any time from Settings. This means you're
never stuck if a provider has an outage, changes pricing, or simply isn't as good at your
particular subject matter.

**Control who can do what.** Out of the box there are two roles: an **Admin** who can do
everything, and a **User** who can take exams and manage their own account — nothing
more. If that's too coarse, create custom roles with exact permissions per area (exam
authoring, taking exams, managing roles, managing users), so a "content author" role can
write exams but never touch user management, for example. The first person to sign up
becomes Admin automatically — no separate setup step required.

## Feature summary

| Area | What you get |
|---|---|
| Exam creation | Multi-section content with per-section weighting; AI-generated questions matched to those weights |
| Editing | Full draft/review/publish workflow; edit or regenerate any single question before publishing |
| Taking exams | Optional countdown timer with auto-submit, or a stopwatch; optional shuffle of questions and answers; resumable if interrupted |
| Results | Score, time metadata, per-section score breakdown, full answer review, CSV export, printable PDF |
| Progress tracking | Every attempt saved; retake exams and see your trend over time |
| Sharing | Publish a link so others can take your exam |
| Access control | Admin / User predefined roles, plus fully custom roles with granular permissions |
| AI provider | Switchable between Claude and Gemini per user |
